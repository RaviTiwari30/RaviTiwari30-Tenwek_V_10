Interaction Effect

diclofenac sodium causes additive toxicity with ciprofloxacin

Severity Level

Moderate -

Documentation Level

Limited -

Probable Mechanism

Coadministration of NSAIDs and quinolones may increase the risk of CNS stimulation and convulsions by synergistically inhibiting GABA (inhibitory neurotransmitter) binding to its receptors. A high incidence of convulsions has been reported in patients on combination of enoxacin and fenbufen, hence this combination should be avoided. Reports of adverse reactions between other quinolones and NSAIDs have been rare, however it would be prudent to monitor for CNS adverse effects (e.g. seizures, tremors). Avoid this combination in patient with risk factors (e.g. elderly, nephropathy, history of seizure) if possible.

Actions to be taken

  1. Use combination with caution.
  2. Monitor for signs of drug toxicity.
